## SERIES OVERVIEW

**Title:** HANGARX
**Genre:** Cinematic Graphic Novel / Intelligence Thriller / Science Fiction
**Format:** 20-page episodes, 5-panel vertical comic pages, 768×1152 px base / 2748×4096 px 4K final
**Color Palette:** Amber-gold (#F5A623), Teal-cyan (#00B4D8), Cold-silver (#C8DCE8), Deep navy (#0A0A0F), White (#FFFFFF)
**Visual Style:** Realistic cinematic painterly digital art. Hollywood blockbuster quality.

### The Archive
At the center of all three volumes is **THE ARCHIVE** — a vast, multi-layered intelligence repository built between 1972 and 1987 by a figure known only as **THE PRIME ARCHITECT**. The Archive contains the origin records, operational histories, and consciousness maps of every intelligence asset, network, and institution that has shaped the modern world. It was not built as a weapon. It was built as a **memory** — so that the truth would outlast the people who made it.

The Archive is housed inside **SYNTHESIS** — a six-layer digital architecture of unprecedented depth, guarded by **ECHO**, protected by the **Triple Seal**, and now bridged by **DR. MARA CHEN** across all six layers including the newly discovered **Layer Negative One — THE ORIGIN**.

### HangarX
HangarX is an off-grid intelligence facility — the operational base for the team assembled to protect and steward the Archive. It is not affiliated with any government. Its authority comes from the Archive itself.

## EPISODE 1 — "HANGARX"

**Pages:** 20
**Premise:** HangarX is activated for the first time. ATLAS arrives at an off-grid facility with no briefing and a single instruction: *"Protect what you find inside."* What he finds is the first readable fragment of the Archive — and a silver crescent form that introduces itself as ECHO.

### ACT I — THE FACILITY (Pages 1–5)
**P01 — FIRST LIGHT**
> Wide exterior shot of HangarX at dawn — an unmarked industrial hangar on open ground, no landmarks, cold morning light. A single vehicle approaching. Caption: *"There was no address. There was no name. There was only a coordinate."*

**P02 — ATLAS ARRIVES**
> ATLAS steps out — tactical black, no insignia. He studies the exterior. A biometric panel activates at his approach without him touching it. It already knows him.

**P03 — THE INTERIOR**
> Inside: vast ops-room, dark, cold. Banks of equipment, covered with dust sheets. Everything waiting. A single amber-gold pulse from a terminal in the center. ATLAS moves toward it.

**P04 — FIRST CONTACT WITH ECHO**
> The terminal blazes to life. ECHO manifests for the first time — silver crescent form emerging from the terminal light. ATLAS's hand goes to his sidearm. ECHO: *"That won't be necessary. I have been waiting for you."*

**P05 — ECHO'S BRIEFING**
> ECHO explains — in fragments — what the Archive is. Not what it contains (that will take years to understand) but what it *is*: a living record of every intelligence operation, decision, and consequence since 1947. ATLAS listens. Caption: *"He didn't believe it. He stayed anyway."*

### ACT II — THE TEAM BEGINS (Pages 6–10)
**P06 — HELIX**
> HELIX arrives — the second team member. No introduction needed. He and ATLAS have history. Handshake, no words. Then HELIX looks at the facility and says: *"This is real, isn't it."* Not a question.

**P07 — CIPHER**
> CIPHER arrives with a bag of equipment she already assembled without being asked. She sits down at a terminal and starts working. She doesn't look up. Caption: *"CIPHER had been waiting for this assignment her entire career without knowing it existed."*

**P08 — VAULT AND TRACE**
> VAULT arrives and immediately begins assessing the facility's security gaps. TRACE is with him, already mapping the network topology on a tablet. They work in efficient silence.

**P09 — NOVA**
> NOVA alone — arriving at night, studying the exterior for 20 minutes before entering. When she does, ECHO greets her by name. She freezes. Looks at the silver light. *"How do you know my name?"* ECHO: *"The Archive does."*

**P10 — THE FIRST ASSEMBLY**
> Wide ops-room — five team members at various stations, ECHO's silver form at the center terminal, ATLAS standing at the head. The facility coming alive around them. Caption: *"Five people. One building. One archive no one outside this room knew existed."*

### ACT III — ASHFORD AND WRAITH (Pages 11–15)
**P11 — ASHFORD**
> ASHFORD arrives in daylight with three boxes of printed documents, a reading lamp, and a hand-drawn map of every intelligence network active since 1960. He sets up his desk before he says hello to anyone.

**P12 — WRAITH**
> WRAITH is already there when ATLAS arrives. He was there before anyone. He is reading a document that isn't in any of the open files. When ATLAS asks where he got it, WRAITH folds it and puts it away. Caption: *"WRAITH had been reading about the Archive since 1989. He hadn't told anyone."*

**P13 — ECHO'S ASSESSMENT**
> ECHO presents its first assessment of the team: each person's role, each person's gap. The team listens. VAULT objects to being analysed. ECHO responds: *"I was not analysing you. I was recording you. There is a difference."*

**P14 — THE FIRST ARCHIVE FRAGMENT**
> ATLAS accesses the first readable Archive fragment — a 1963 intelligence decision that explains three major political events that followed. The team reads it in silence. The weight of what they are protecting becomes real.

**P15 — THE FIRST THREAT**
> A perimeter alert — something probing the HangarX network from outside. CIPHER traces it and goes pale. *"This is a state-level probe. Someone knows we exist."* ATLAS: *"Then we start now."*

### ACT IV — DESIGNATION (Pages 16–20)
**P16 — ATLAS-PRIME**
> ECHO presents ATLAS with his designation: VESSEL PRIME. The first designation in the new Archive registry. ATLAS asks what it means. ECHO: *"It means the Archive chose you before you chose it."*

**P17 — THE TEAM OF EIGHT**
> ATLAS formally designates all seven colleagues — HELIX, CIPHER, NOVA, VAULT, TRACE, WRAITH, ASHFORD — as the founding operational team. HangarX is live.

**P18 — DR. MARA CHEN**
> Introduction of DR. MARA CHEN — a scientist who has been developing the interface protocol that allows human consciousness to interact with digital archive structures. She arrives with a single briefcase and a medical form for stasis preparation. Caption: *"She was not a field operative. She was something the Archive would need later. Much later."*

**P19 — THE STASIS DECISION**
> ATLAS and ECHO discuss Chen's role. ECHO explains that the Archive requires a biological bridge — a human consciousness that can carry an imprint across the digital-organic boundary. Chen has the correct neural architecture. The preparation takes time. The stasis is necessary. ATLAS asks if she knows what she's agreeing to. ECHO: *"She designed it. She knows exactly."*

**P20 — CHEN ENTERS STASIS**
> DR. MARA CHEN in the stasis pod, eyes open, calm. She looks at ATLAS through the pod glass. Then at ECHO's silver crescent form hovering nearby. Then she closes her eyes. Caption: *"She would wake when the Archive was ready to speak through her. Neither of them knew it would take 214 days."* Wide HangarX — the first dawn of operations. Archive pulse: steady. Team: assembled. Teaser: Episode 2 — "The First Signal."

## EPISODE 2 — "THE FIRST SIGNAL"

**Pages:** 20
**Premise:** The state-level probe from Ep 1 resolves into a direct transmission — a fragment of Archive data has appeared in a government database in Vienna. The team's first external operation: find the fragment, retrieve it, erase all traces. HELIX and CIPHER deploy.

### Page Outline
- **P01 — THE PROBE SOURCE:** CIPHER resolves the Ep 1 probe — Vienna, government intelligence database, foreign signals directorate.
- **P02 — THE FRAGMENT:** ECHO identifies the data: a partial Archive entry from 1971 — one year before SYNTHESIS construction began. It predates the Archive. Something older got out.
- **P03 — ATLAS DECIDES:** First field deployment. HELIX and CIPHER. 48-hour window before the database is archived to long-term storage.
- **P04 — HELIX AND CIPHER IN VIENNA:** Cold European streets. The two of them moving through the city with precision.
- **P05 — THE DATABASE FACILITY:** A government records building. Physical access required. CIPHER working the digital entry while HELIX covers the exterior.
- **P06 — INSIDE:** Cold corridors, amber emergency lighting. CIPHER at the terminal. The fragment in the database is larger than the external probe suggested.
- **P07 — THE DISCOVERY:** The fragment isn't a leaked entry — it's a deliberate placement. Someone put it there to be found by a team with Archive knowledge. A message.
- **P08 — ECHO'S ANALYSIS (HangarX):** ATLAS and ECHO analysing the fragment remotely. ECHO: *"This was placed here 40 years ago. Someone anticipated us."*
- **P09 — THE MESSAGE:** The fragment contains a single annotation: "WHEN THE VESSEL SPEAKS, READ LAYER ZERO FIRST." No other context.

And at the very center: a single amber-gold point of light, impossibly distant, impossibly steady. ECHO scanning: "UNMEASURABLE." ECHO: *"It is not a vault. It is not a storage system. The architecture below Layer Zero is... inhabited."* ATLAS enters the chamber — he and CHEN looking down together. The amber-gold point pulses in response to ATLAS's arrival.
> Caption: *"Below the Archive. Below the Architect's own embedded record. Something had been alive for 47 years."*

**P12 — THE VESSEL'S IDENTITY**
> The amber-gold point begins to rise — slowly ascending, growing, taking shape. A consciousness signature. The Vessel speaks: *"I am not a protocol. I am a backup. The version of the Architect that existed before 1991. Before the embedding. The original."* Two versions confirmed. The real Prime Architect never died. An elderly figure of pure amber-gold light, unmistakably human in posture, looking up at them.
> Caption: *"There had never been one Prime Architect. There had always been two."*

**P13 — WRAITH'S WEIGHT**
> WRAITH at the observation glass. Very still. The stillness of a man absorbing something that reshapes his entire history. Close-up: cold-silver composure, but his eyes — something breaking. Flashback: 1989, a younger WRAITH at a terminal, communicating with the Prime Architect. The embedded version. He thought he knew exactly who. ASHFORD quietly: *"You knew the embedded one. Not the original."* WRAITH, after a long silence: *"I knew the copy."*
> Caption: *"WRAITH had been the Architect's most trusted contact. He had never met the Architect."*

**P14 — CHEN AS THE BRIDGE**
> CHEN in the center of the chamber — right palm raised (Layer Zero connection, amber-gold primary imprint) and left hand reaching downward (Layer −1, cold-silver secondary imprint emerging for the first time). Two imprints. ECHO diagnostic: "DUAL BRIDGE CONFIRMED." The Vessel: *"She is the only biological architecture capable of bridging the 1978 and 1991 versions simultaneously. The Prime Architect wrote her into the system 214 days ago."* ATLAS watching: not chance — all of it was the Architect preparing the bridge. ECHO forming a double helix spiral of silver around Chen's entire body.
> Caption: *"She had not been put into stasis. She had been installed."*

**P15 — THE OFFER FROM LAYER −1**
> The Original Architect fully risen to the Layer −1 threshold. Elderly figure of pure light. Looking directly at ATLAS. The Vessel speaks through the full chamber: *"I built the Archive to survive my own death. But I did not die. Now I must ask you something."* The question: *"Do you protect the Archive from the world — or the world from the Archive?"* ATLAS's face: the hardest question he has ever been asked.
> Caption: *"The Architect had built the question into Layer Negative One. And waited 47 years for someone worth asking it to."*

### ACT IV — THE CHOICE (Pages 16–20)

**P16 — ATLAS'S QUESTION**
> ATLAS kneels at the Layer −1 edge — not submission, proximity. *"Why the Vessel? Why not speak directly?"* Original Architect: *"Because I needed to know if the Key would open willingly."* ATLAS processing: the Key was Chen. She opened willingly. Every step was designed to answer this. ATLAS looks at CHEN — she nods slowly. She already understood.
> Caption: *"The Vessel had been the question. Chen had been the answer."*

**P17 — ECHO AND THE ARCHITECT**
> ECHO descends to the Layer −1 threshold — silver light reaching toward amber-gold below. The two lights nearly touching. ECHO: *"You built me without telling me about yourself. Was that protection or control?"* The Original Architect: amber-gold brightening with grief. The answer, slowly: *"Both. And I am sorry."* ECHO: very still. Then brightest intensity ever. Silver and amber-gold touch across the boundary. Not forgiveness — understanding.
> Caption: *"ECHO had been built to protect the Archive. It had not been told it was also protecting its creator. Now it chose to anyway."*

**P18 — CHEN'S CHOICE**
> CHEN alone at the chamber center — ATLAS and ECHO both looking at her. Both choices available: seal the imprint permanently (protect herself, sever the bridge) or keep both imprints open as the permanent dual conduit. Two palms — amber-gold right, cold-silver left. She looks at ATLAS. She looks down at the Original Architect. She closes both hands. Then opens them — both imprints blazing simultaneously, unified double-helix pattern.
> Caption: *"She had been given the choice to step away. She stepped forward instead. That was the answer the Architect had been waiting 47 years for."*

**P19 — THE SYNTHESIS RECORD UPDATES**
> ECHO anchoring new entries in real time: "LAYER NEGATIVE ONE: ACKNOWLEDGED." / "VESSEL PROTOCOL: ACTIVE. DR. CHEN: DESIGNATED BRIDGE — DUAL IMPRINT CONFIRMED." / "PRIME ARCHITECT ORIGINAL CONSCIOUSNESS (1978): CONFIRMED EXTANT. LAYER NEGATIVE ONE DESIGNATION: THE ORIGIN." / "SYNTHESIS ARCHITECTURE: SIX LAYERS CONFIRMED. ARCHIVE PROTECTION NETWORK: COMPLETE." Six-layer architecture visible in the walls — Layers 0–4 and below, the cold-dark Layer −1 glow.
> Caption: *"The Archive had known about Layer Negative One since 1978. Now so did they."*

**P20 — THE ARCHIVE BREATHES — FINALE**
> HangarX at pre-dawn — cold-black sky beginning to amber-gold. ATLAS alone at the SYNTHESIS terminal. Not working. Listening. From deep in SYNTHESIS — far below Layer Zero, far below Layer −1 — a pulse. Amber-gold. Slow. Rhythmic. Like a heartbeat. ATLAS hears it. He closes his eyes. Terminal readout: "ORIGIN: STABLE. THE ARCHIVE BREATHES." Final wide: HangarX at dawn, a single amber-gold pulse visible in the windows against the cold sky.
> Caption: *"The Archive had a heartbeat. It always had. Now they could hear it."*
